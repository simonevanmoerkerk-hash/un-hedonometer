# Emotional Tone of UN General Debate Speeches: China vs USA (1972–2025): Applying the labMT Hedometer

## Project Overview

## Research Question

How has the emotional tone of China's (PRC) UN General Debate speeches evolved since joining the United Nations in 1971, and how does that trajectory compare to the United States over the same period?

This question matters for Digital Humanities as it uses computational methods to trace how two global superpowers present themselves on the world stage through language. The UN General Debate is one of the few forums where every country speaks in a comparable format every year. Which makes it the ideal corpus for longitudinal comparison. By applying the labMT hedonometer to 54 years of diplomatic speech, this project asks whether shifts in global power are reflected in shifts in linguistic tone, and whether a sentiment tool built from American English captures those shifts equally for both countries.

## Key Findings

China's UN speeches show a clear upward trend in emotional tone from 1972 to 2025, starting low (~5.28) when it first joined the UN and rising to consistently above 5.5 by the 2000s. The United States, by contrast, shows greater volatility and a downward trend — particularly after 2001 and again after 2017. The two countries effectively swap positions around 2000, with China scoring higher than the USA for most of the post-2000 period. This difference is statistically significant (bootstrap 95% CI: [0.0073, 0.0625], p < 0.05).
